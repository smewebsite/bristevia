PART 1

Article I. Time When the Act Takes Effect.

This Code shall take effect immediately upon the **Imperial Signature** of His Majesty the Souverion and Her Majesty the Souveria, and its promulgation throughout the Imperial Realm of Bristevia.

Article II. Application of Its Provisions.

Except as may be provided under treaties, compacts, or laws of preferential application, the provisions of this Code shall have force and effect throughout the entire **Dominion of Bristevia** — encompassing all provinces, dependencies, territories, and Imperial dominions, including its atmosphere, interior waters, and maritime jurisdiction.

This Code shall likewise apply beyond the Imperial boundaries to any person who:

  1. Commits an offense aboard an Imperial ship or airship registered under the flag of Bristevia;
  2. Forges or counterfeits any coin, currency, obligation, or security issued by the Imperial Treasury or any instrument of the Crown;
  3. Participates in the introduction or circulation of any such falsified obligations or securities within the Empire;
  4. Being an officer or servant of the Crown, commits an offense in the exercise of his official functions; or
  5. Commits crimes against the Imperial Crown, Imperial Security, or the Law of Nations, as defined in this Code.

TITLE ONE: Of Felonies and the Circumstances Which Affect Criminal Liability

CHAPTER ONE — Of Felonies

Article III. Definition.

Acts and omissions punishable by Imperial law are denominated felonies (delitos imperiales).

Felonies are committed either:

  • By means of **deceit (dolo)**, when the act is done with deliberate intent; or
  • By **fault (culpa)**, when the wrongful act results from imprudence, negligence, lack of foresight, or want of skill.
Article IV. Criminal Liability.

Criminal liability shall be incurred:

  1. By any person committing a felony, although the wrongful act done be **different from that which he intended**;
  2. By any person performing an act which would constitute an offense against person or property were it not for the inherent **impossibility of its accomplishment** or the ineffectual means employed.
Article V. Duty of the Courts.

Whenever an Imperial Court perceives an act proper to repress yet **not punishable by any law**, it shall decide the matter accordingly and submit a report to the Minister of Justice, detailing the grounds for recommending penal legislation.

Likewise, where strict application of this Code results in a clearly **excessive penalty**, the Court shall report the matter to the Minister of Justice for review, without suspending the execution of judgment.

Article VI. Degrees of Felony.

Felonies are **consummated, frustrated, or attempted**, all of which are punishable.

  • A felony is **consummated** when all elements necessary for its execution are present.
  • It is **frustrated** when all acts of execution are performed but the felony is not produced by causes independent of the perpetrator’s will.
  • It is **attempted** when the offender begins the commission by overt acts but fails to complete it due to causes other than voluntary desistance.
Article VII. When Light Felonies are Punishable.

**Light felonies** are punishable only when **consummated**, except when committed against persons or property.

Article VIII. Conspiracy and Proposal.

**Conspiracy and proposal** to commit felony are punishable only when **expressly provided by law**.

  • **Conspiracy** exists when two or more persons agree and decide to commit a felony.
  • **Proposal** exists when a person who has resolved to commit a felony proposes its execution to another.
Article IX. Classification of Felonies.
  • **Grave felonies** are those punishable by capital or afflictive penalties.
  • **Less grave felonies** are punished by correctional penalties.
  • **Light felonies** are those punishable by minor arrest or fine as set by this Code.
Article X. Offenses Under Special Imperial Laws.

Offenses punishable under special Imperial enactments are **not governed by this Code** unless expressly provided. This Code shall serve as **supplementary law** thereto.

CHAPTER TWO — Justifying and Exempting Circumstances

Article XI. Justifying Circumstances.

No criminal liability shall arise from:

  1. **Defense of one’s person or rights**, when there is:
    • Unlawful aggression;
    • Reasonable necessity of means to repel it;
    • Lack of sufficient provocation on the part of the defender.
  2. **Defense of spouse, ascendants, descendants, legitimate or adopted siblings, or relatives** within the fourth degree, under the same conditions.
  3. **Defense of a stranger**, under the first two requisites, provided the act is not driven by revenge or evil motive.
  4. The **avoidance of a greater evil**, provided:
    • The evil sought to be avoided actually exists;
    • The injury feared is greater than that done to prevent it;
    • No other less harmful means are available.
  5. **Fulfillment of a duty** or lawful exercise of right or office.
  6. **Obedience to a lawful order** issued by a superior.
Article XII. Exempting Circumstances.

The following are exempt from criminal liability:

  1. **Imbeciles or insane persons**, unless the latter act during a lucid interval.
  2. Persons **under fifteen** years old.
  3. Persons **over fifteen but under eighteen**, unless they act with discernment.
  4. Persons causing injury by pure **accident** in the performance of a lawful act with due care.
  5. Persons acting under **irresistible force**.
  6. Persons acting under **uncontrollable fear** of an equal or greater injury.
  7. Persons prevented by **lawful cause** from performing an act required by law.

CHAPTER THREE — Mitigating Circumstances

Article XIII. Mitigating Circumstances.

The following circumstances lessen criminal liability:

  1. **Incomplete justifying or exempting** circumstances;
  2. Offender **under eighteen or over seventy** years of age;
  3. **No intention** to commit so grave a wrong;
  4. **Sufficient provocation** from the offended party;
  5. Immediate **vindication of a grave offense**;
  6. Impulse of **passion or obfuscation**;
  7. **Voluntary surrender** or confession before trial;
  8. **Physical defect** restricting defense or communication;
  9. **Illness** diminishing willpower without loss of reason;
  10. Any **analogous circumstance** of equal mitigating character.

CHAPTER FOUR — Aggravating Circumstances

Article XIV. Aggravating Circumstances.

The following circumstances increase criminal liability:

  1. **Abuse of public office** or position of trust;
  2. **Contempt or insult to Imperial authorities**;
  3. **Disregard of respect** due to rank, age, or sex; or offense committed in the **dwelling** of the offended party without provocation;
  4. **Abuse of confidence** or evident ungratefulness;
  5. Crime committed within the **Imperial Palace**, in presence of the Sovereigns, or in places of worship or public duty;
  6. Crime committed **by night**, in uninhabited places, or by a **band** of armed persons;
  7. Crime committed during **conflagration, storm, epidemic**, or similar calamity;
  8. Crime committed with aid of **armed men** or with **assurance of impunity**;
  9. **Habitual delinquency or recidivism**;
  10. **Previous punishment** for equal or greater offense;
  11. Crime committed for **price, reward, or promise**;
  12. Crime committed by **destructive means** such as fire, poison, explosion, or inundation;
  13. **Evident premeditation**;
  14. Use of **craft, fraud, or disguise**;
  15. **Abuse of superior strength** or employment of means to weaken defense;
  16. **Treachery** to ensure execution without risk to oneself;
  17. Adding **ignominy** to the natural effects of the act;
  18. **Unlawful entry** by improper means;
  19. Breaking wall, roof, floor, door, or window to commit offense;
  20. Use of minors, motorized vehicles, or airships as means of offense;
  21. **Deliberate augmentation of wrong** beyond necessity.

CHAPTER FIVE — Alternative Circumstances

Article XV. Definition.

Alternative circumstances may aggravate or mitigate depending on the conditions of the act. These include:

  • **Relationship** between offender and offended party;
  • **Intoxication**;
  • Degree of **education or instruction**.

**Intoxication** is mitigating when not habitual or premeditated; otherwise, it is aggravating.

TITLE TWO: Persons Criminally Liable

Article XVI. Criminal Liability.

For grave and less grave felonies, the following are criminally liable:

  1. Principals;
  2. Accomplices;
  3. Accessories.

For light felonies, only **Principals and Accomplices** are liable.

Article XVII. Principals.

Those are principals who:

  1. Take **direct part in the execution**;
  2. **Induce others** to commit;
  3. Cooperate by **indispensable acts**.
Article XVIII. Accomplices.

Those who cooperate in the execution of the offense by **previous or simultaneous acts**, without being principals.

Article XIX. Accessories.

Those who, after knowing of the crime, participate by:

  1. **Profiting** from its effects;
  2. **Concealing evidence** or effects of the crime;
  3. **Harboring or assisting** the principal, especially in crimes against the Crown, murder, or treason.
Article XX. Exempt Accessories.

Accessories related to the principal within the degree of **spouse, ascendant, descendant, or sibling** shall not be punished, except those profiting from the crime.

TITLE THREE: Of Penalties

CHAPTER ONE — General Principles

Article XXI. Legality of Penalties.

No person shall be punished by any penalty **not prescribed by Imperial law** prior to the commission of the felony.

Article XXII. Retroactivity.

Penal laws **favorable to the offender shall apply retroactively** unless the offender is a habitual criminal.

Article XXIII. Effect of Pardon.

A pardon by the offended party **does not extinguish criminal liability**, except when the law expressly provides; but it extinguishes civil liability upon express waiver.

Article XXIV. Preventive or Safety Measures.

The following are **not considered penalties** under Imperial law:

  1. Preventive detention of accused persons;
  2. Commitment of minors to reformative institutions;
  3. Suspension from office pending trial;
  4. Administrative fines;
  5. Civil reparations as may be established by Imperial civil law.

CHAPTER TWO — Classification of Penalties

Article XXV. Imperial Scale of Penalties

Principal Penalties

  • **Capital**: Death (Imperial Execution)
  • **Afflictive**: Reclusion Perpetua, Reclusion Temporal, Absolute or Special Disqualification (Perpetual or Temporary), Prisión Mayor
  • **Correctional**: Prisión Correccional, Arresto Mayor, Suspension, or Destierro (Banishment)
  • **Light**: Arresto Menor, Public Censure
  • **Common Penalties**: Fine or Bond to Keep the Peace

Accessory Penalties

  • Disqualification (Absolute or Special)
  • Suspension of rights or offices
  • Civil interdiction
  • Indemnification
  • Forfeiture or confiscation
  • Payment of costs

PART 2 — Duration, Effects, and Pecuniary Penalties

CHAPTER THREE — Duration and Effect of Penalties

SECTION ONE — Duration of Penalties

Article XXVI. Fine — Currency of Bris

Fine is classified according to the amount:

  • Afflictive penalty: > 6,000 Bris
  • Correctional penalty: 200–6,000 Bris
  • Light penalty: < 200 Bris
Article XXVII. Duration of Principal Penalties
  • Reclusion perpetua: 30 years, unless pardon denied for serious cause.
  • Reclusion temporal: 12 years + 1 day to 20 years.
  • Prisión mayor / Temporary disqualification: 6 years + 1 day to 12 years.
  • Prisión correccional / Suspension / Destierro: 6 months + 1 day to 6 years.
  • Arresto mayor: 1 month + 1 day to 6 months.
  • Arresto menor: 1 day to 30 days.
  • Bond to keep the peace: Duration as determined by the court.
Article XXVIII. Computation of Penalties

If imprisoned: counted from final judgment.

If free: counted from placement under judicial custody.

Other penalties: counted from commencement of sentence.

Article XXIX. Preventive Imprisonment Deduction

Half of preventive detention credited toward sentence, except:

  • Recidivists or ≥2 prior convictions
  • Failure to surrender voluntarily
  • Conviction for robbery, theft, estafa, public fund malversation, falsification, vagrancy, or prostitution

SECTION TWO — Effects of Penalties

Article XXX. Perpetual / Temporary Absolute Disqualification
  • Loss of office or employment, even if elected
  • Loss of right to vote or hold office
  • Disqualification from offices or employment during penalty
  • Loss of retirement or pension rights
Article XXXI. Perpetual / Temporary Special Disqualification
  • Loss of affected office, profession, or calling
  • Disqualification for similar offices as per duration
Article XXXII. Special Disqualification — Suffrage
  • Loss of right to vote or be elected per duration
  • Cannot hold office during disqualification
Article XXXIII. Suspension
  • Disqualifies from holding office or profession and exercising suffrage during sentence
  • Cannot hold similar office while suspended
Article XXXIV. Civil Interdiction

Loss of parental/guardian authority, marital authority, and property management/disposal rights.

Article XXXV. Bond to Keep the Peace

Two sufficient sureties or deposit to guarantee no offense. Failure results in detention:

  • ≤6 months (grave/less grave felony)
  • ≤30 days (light felony)
Article XXXVI. Pardon

Does not restore office or suffrage unless explicitly stated. Does not exempt civil indemnity.

Article XXXVII. Costs

Include all judicial fees and indemnities, fixed or variable by law.

Article XXXVIII. Pecuniary Liabilities — Order of Payment
  1. Reparation of damage caused
  2. Indemnification of consequential damages
  3. Fine
  4. Costs of proceedings
Article XXXIX. Subsidiary Penalty

No property → 1 day per 2.50 Bris. Rules:

  • Prison + fine: subsidiary ≤ 1/3 of sentence, max 1 year
  • Fine only: subsidiary ≤ 6 months (grave/less grave), ≤15 days (light)
  • Higher than prisión correccional: no subsidiary
  • Fixed non-confinement penalties: same deprivations applied
  • Insolvency does not relieve from damage or indemnity; fine may be relieved

SECTION THREE — Accessory Penalties

Article XL. Death

Perpetual absolute disqualification + civil interdiction 30 years unless remitted.

Article XLI. Reclusion Perpetua / Temporal

Civil interdiction + perpetual absolute disqualification.

Article XLII. Prisión Mayor

Temporary absolute disqualification + perpetual special disqualification (suffrage).

Article XLIII. Prisión Correccional

Suspension from office/profession + perpetual special disqualification if >18 months.

Article XLIV. Arresto

Suspension from office + suffrage during term.

Article XLV. Confiscation / Forfeiture

Crime proceeds and instruments forfeited to Government; destroyed if not lawful commerce.

PART 2 — Continuation: Application and Execution of Penalties, Extinction of Liability, and Civil Liability

CHAPTER FOUR — Application of Penalties

Article XLVI. Application to Principals

Penalties for principals shall follow the law. General penalties apply to consummated felonies.

Article XLVII. Limitations on Death Penalty

Death penalty is not imposed if:

  • Offender is over seventy years old
  • Supreme Tribunal is not unanimous on imposition
Article XLVIII. Complex Crimes

Apply penalty for the most serious crime at its maximum.

Article XLIX. Wrongful Act Differs from Intended Felony

Higher prescribed penalty → apply penalty for intended felony at maximum.
Lower prescribed penalty → apply penalty for committed felony at maximum.
Attempt or frustration of another crime → apply higher prescribed penalty.

Article L. Frustrated Felony

Penalty one degree lower than consummated felony.

Article LI. Attempted Felony

Penalty two degrees lower than consummated felony.

Article LII. Accomplices in Consummated Crime

Penalty is one degree lower than that of the principal.

Article LIII. Accessories in Consummated Crime

Penalty is two degrees lower than that of the principal.

Article LIV. Accomplices in Frustrated Crime

Penalty is one degree lower than that of the principal.

Article LV. Accessories in Frustrated Crime

Penalty is two degrees lower than that of the principal.

Article LVI. Accomplices in Attempted Crime

Penalty is one degree lower than that of the principal.

Article LVII. Accessories in Attempted Crime

Penalty is two degrees lower than that of the principal.

Article LVIII. Certain Accessories Abusing Office
  • Absolute perpetual disqualification if principal guilty of grave felony.
  • Absolute temporary disqualification if principal guilty of less grave felony.
Article LIX. Impossible Crime

Penalty: Arresto mayor or fine 200–500 Bris.

Article LX. Specific Penalties Override General Rules

Articles 50–57 do not apply if the law prescribes specific penalties for attempts or accomplices.

Article LXI. Graduation of Penalties

Follow scale in Article 25; adjust according to indivisible/divisible penalties as needed.

SECTION TWO — Mitigating, Aggravating Circumstances, and Habitual Delinquency

Article LXII. Application of Circumstances
  • Aggravating circumstances inherent to the crime are ignored.
  • Circumstances based on offender’s personal relations or morals modify penalties for principals, accomplices, and accessories.
  • Habitual delinquency:
    • 3rd conviction → last crime penalty + prisión correccional (medium & max).
    • 4th conviction → last crime penalty + prisión mayor (min & medium).
    • 5th+ conviction → last crime penalty + prisión mayor (max) to reclusion temporal (min).
  • Total penalty ≤ 30 years.
Article LXIII. Indivisible Penalties
  • One aggravating circumstance → greater penalty.
  • No circumstances → lesser penalty.
  • Both present → offset reasonably.
Article LXIV. Penalties with Three Periods
  • None → medium period.
  • Mitigating only → minimum.
  • Aggravating only → maximum.
  • Both → offset by weight.
  • Multiple mitigating → next lower penalty.
  • Maximum penalty cannot exceed law.
Article LXV. Non-Three Period Penalties

Divide into minimum, medium, and maximum portions.

Article LXVI. Fines

Adjust within legal limits; consider offender’s means and circumstances.

Article LXVII. Partial Fulfillment of Exempting Circumstance #4 (Art. 12)
  • Grave felony → arresto mayor max to prisión correccional min.
  • Less grave → arresto mayor min & medium.
Article LXVIII. Offender Under 18
  • Age 10–15 → discretionary penalty, at least 2 degrees lower.
  • Age 15–18 → next lower penalty in proper period.
Article LXIX. Partially Excusable Acts

Penalty 1–2 degrees lower; court sets proper period.

Article LXX. Successive Service of Penalties

Serve simultaneously if possible; otherwise, order by severity:

  1. Death
  2. Reclusion perpetua
  3. Reclusion temporal
  4. Prisión mayor
  5. Prisión correccional
  6. Arresto mayor
  7. Arresto menor
  8. Destierro served last if combined with other penalties
Article LXXI. Fine as Last Principal Penalty

Subsidiary term ≤ limits in Art. 39.

Article LXXII. Civil Liabilities

Pay in chronological order.

SECTION THREE — Accessory Penalties

Article LXXIII. Accessory Penalties

Articles 40–45 automatically included with principal penalties.

Article LXXIV. Penalty Higher than Reclusion Perpetua

Treated as death with Art. 40 accessories.

Article LXXV. Adjust Fines ±1 Degree

Adjustment = ±25% of maximum; minimum unchanged.

Article LXXVI. Divisible Penalties

Divided into minimum, medium, and maximum periods (see table in Art. 76).

Article LXXVII. Three-Part Complex Penalties

Assign lightest → min, next → medium, heaviest → max; use analogy if needed.

CHAPTER FIVE — Execution and Service of Penalties

SECTION ONE — General Provisions

Article LXXVIII. General Rule

Execute penalties only by final judgment, following law and institutional regulations. Sexes separated; reform and correction prioritized.

Article LXXIX. Insane or Imbecile Convicts

Suspend personal penalties; execute if restored to reason.

Article LXXX. Minors Under 18

Suspend proceedings; commit to care or institutions; monitor progress; return to court for final release or judgment if misbehavior occurs. Costs borne by guardians if able.

PART 2 — Continuation: Execution, Extinction of Liability, and Civil Liability

SECTION TWO — Execution of Principal Penalties

Article LXXXI. Death

Executed first, by electrocution; offender may request anesthesia; Director of Prisons ensures minimal suffering.

Article LXXXII. Execution Day

Set by court; notify ≥8 hours prior; offender may consult clergy, lawyers, family, or make a will.

Article LXXXIII. Death Suspensions

Suspended for women <3 yrs post-sentence, pregnant persons, and persons >70; commuted to reclusion perpetua with Art. 40 accessories.

Article LXXXIV. Execution Location

Closed penitentiary; witnesses limited to clergy, lawyers, relatives (≤6), physician, and prison staff.

Article LXXXV. Corpse

Family may claim; otherwise, given to research institution or buried by Director at government expense; no pomp.

Article LXXXVI. Other Principal Penalties

Reclusion perpetua, temporal, prisión mayor/correccional, arresto mayor: Serve in designated penal institutions.

Article LXXXVII. Destierro

Offender barred 25–250 km from specified area.

Article LXXXVIII. Arresto Menor

Served in municipal jail or offender’s home under supervision.

TITLE FOUR — Extinction of Criminal Liability

CHAPTER ONE — Total Extinction

Article LXXXIX. Total Extinction
  • Death of the convict (personal penalties); pecuniary penalties extinguished only if death occurs before final judgment.
  • Service of sentence.
  • Amnesty (fully extinguishes penalty and effects).
  • Absolute pardon.
  • Prescription of the crime.
  • Prescription of the penalty.
  • Marriage of offended woman (Art. 344).
Article XC. Prescription of Crimes
  • Death, reclusion perpetua, reclusion temporal → 20 years
  • Other afflictive → 15 years
  • Correctional → 10 years; arresto mayor → 5 years
  • Libel → 2 years
  • Oral defamation / slander → 6 months
  • Light offenses → 2 months
  • Compound penalties → use highest penalty as reference
Article XCI. Prescription Computation

Runs from discovery of crime; interrupted by complaint or proceedings; resumes if proceedings terminate without conviction. Excluded if offender is absent from Bristevia.

Article XCII. Prescription of Penalties
  • Death, reclusion perpetua → 20 years
  • Other afflictive → 15 years
  • Correctional → 10 years; arresto mayor → 5 years
  • Light → 1 year
Article XCIII. Computation of Penalties

Starts when offender should begin sentence; interrupted if offender surrenders, is captured, flees abroad without treaty, or commits another crime.

Article XCIV. Partial Extinction
  • Conditional pardon
  • Commutation of sentence
  • Good conduct allowances
Article XCV. Conditional Pardon

Offender must comply strictly with conditions; non-compliance → revocation, apply Art. 159.

Article XCVI. Commutation

Substitute new sentence for original.

Article XCVII. Good Conduct Allowance
  • Years 1–2 → 5 days/month
  • Years 3–5 → 8 days/month
  • Years 6–10 → 10 days/month
  • Year 11+ → 15 days/month
Article XCVIII. Loyalty Allowance

1/5 deduction if offender surrenders within 48 hours after calamity proclamation.

Article XCIX. Director of Prisons

Grants time allowances; once granted, not revoked.

TITLE FIVE — Civil Liability

CHAPTER ONE — Persons Civilly Liable

Article C. General Rule

Criminally liable persons are also civilly liable.

Article CI. Civil Liability Rules

Age <15 exempt from criminal liability, but civil liability enforced:

  • Imbeciles, insane, or persons <15 acting without discernment → civil liability devolves on legal guardians; if none, on offender’s property (except exempt property).
  • Beneficiaries of prevented harm → liable proportionately.
  • Subdivisions 5 & 6 (irresistible force / uncontrollable fear) → primary liability on actor; secondary liability if no primary actor.
Article CII. Innkeepers, Tavernkeepers, Proprietors

Subsidiarily liable for crimes in establishments; restitution for theft only if guest notified and care instructions followed.

Article CIII. Employers, Teachers, Supervisors

Subsidiarily liable for crimes of subordinates committed in duty.

CHAPTER TWO — Scope of Civil Liability

Article CIV. Scope
  • Restitution
  • Reparation of damage
  • Indemnification for consequential damages
Article CV. Restitution

Restore thing itself if possible; if in third party’s possession, restitution still enforced.

Article CVI. Reparation

Court sets damage amount, considering value and sentimental worth.

Article CVII. Indemnification

Covers injury to victim, family, or third parties caused by crime.

Article CVIII. Obligations of Heirs

Heirs of offender and heirs of injured party inherit rights/duties for restoration, reparation, indemnification.

Article CIX. Multiple Liable Persons

Court determines each share.

Article CX. Principals, Accomplices, Accessories

Liable severally (in solidum) within class, subsidiarily for others. Enforcement order: principals → accomplices → accessories. Payment gives right of action against others for shares.

Article CXI. Gratuitous Participants

Obliged to restore equivalent share.

CHAPTER THREE — Extinction & Survival

Article CXII. Extinction

Civil liability extinguished like other obligations (Civil Law).

Article CXIII. Survival of Civil Liability

Offender remains obliged to satisfy civil liability even if sentence served, commuted, pardoned, or not required due to amnesty or other reasons.

⚜️ PART 3 – BOOK TWO: CRIMES AND PENALTIES ⚜️

TITLE ONE — Crimes Against National Security and the Law of Nations

CHAPTER ONE — Treason, Espionage, and Related Crimes

Article CXIV. Treason

Any person owing allegiance to the Empire of Bristevia who levies war against it or aids its enemies, within or outside the Empire, shall suffer reclusion temporal to death and a fine ≤ 20,000 Bris. Conviction requires two witnesses to the same overt act or confession in open court.

Article CXV. Conspiracy / Proposal to Commit Treason
  • Conspiracy → prision mayor + fine ≤ 10,000 Bris
  • Proposal → prision correccional + fine ≤ 5,000 Bris
Article CXVI. Misprision of Treason

Knowledge of treason and concealment → accessory to treason.

Article CXVII. Espionage
  • Prision correccional for unauthorized entry to military/naval installations to obtain confidential information.
  • Disclosure of such information to foreign powers.
  • If offender is public officer → penalty next higher in degree.
Article CXVIII. Inciting War / Reprisals
  • Public officer → reclusion temporal
  • Private individual → prision mayor
  • Acts provoking war or exposing citizens to reprisals.
Article CXIX. Violation of Neutrality

Prision correccional for violating neutrality regulations during foreign war.

Article CXX. Correspondence with Hostile Country
  • Prohibited → prision correccional
  • In cipher → prision mayor
  • Gives useful notice → reclusion temporal
  • Intends to aid enemy → reclusion temporal to death
Article CXXI. Flight to Enemy Country

Arresto mayor for attempting to flee when prohibited.

CHAPTER TWO — Piracy and Mutiny

Article CXXII. Piracy / Mutiny on High Seas

Reclusion temporal for attacks or seizure of vessels/cargo, or mutiny.

Article CXXIII. Qualified Piracy

Reclusion temporal to death if:

  • Vessel seized by boarding / firing
  • Victims abandoned
  • Accompanied by murder, homicide, physical injuries, or rape

TITLE TWO — Crimes Against Fundamental Laws of the State

CHAPTER ONE — Arbitrary Detention, Domicile Violation, Peaceful Assemblies, and Religious Worship

Article CXXIV. Arbitrary Detention

Public officer, without legal ground:

  • ≤3 days → arresto mayor max → prision correccional min
  • 4–15 days → prision correccional medium/max
  • 16 days–6 months → prision mayor
  • 6 months+ → reclusion temporal
Article CXXV. Delay in Delivery to Judicial Authority

Same penalties as Art. 124.

Article CXXVI. Delaying Release

Same penalties as Art. 124.

Article CXXVII. Expulsion

Prision correccional for unauthorized expulsion or forced relocation.

Article CXXVIII. Violation of Domicile

Public officer entering dwelling without authorization → prision correccional min. Nighttime or failure to return effects → prision correccional medium/max.

Article CXXIX. Malicious / Abusive Search Warrants

Arresto mayor max → prision correccional min + fine ≤ 1,000 Bris.

Article CXXX. Searching Without Witnesses

Arresto mayor medium/max if done without required witnesses.

Article CXXXI. Prohibition / Interruption of Peaceful Meetings

Prision correccional min; hindrance → same; preventing petitions → same.

Article CXXXII. Interruption of Religious Worship

Prision correccional min; with violence/threats → medium/max.

Article CXXXIII. Offending Religious Feelings

Arresto mayor max → prision correccional min for offensive acts in places of worship or ceremonies.

TITLE THREE — Crimes Against Public Order

CHAPTER ONE — Rebellion, Sedition, and Disloyalty

Article CXXXIV. Rebellion / Insurrection

Public rising / armed acts against Empire to remove authority.

Article CXXXV. Penalty

Leaders / promoters → prision mayor + fine ≤ 20,000 Bris. Participants → prision mayor min.

Article CXXXVI. Conspiracy / Proposal
  • Conspiracy → prision correccional max + fine ≤ 5,000 Bris
  • Proposal → prision correccional medium + fine ≤ 2,000 Bris

CHAPTER THREE — Seduction, Corruption, White Slave Trade

Article CCCXXXVII. Qualified Seduction

Minor 12–18, authority/trust → prision correccional min/med; higher degree if seducing sister/descendant.

Article CCCXXXVIII. Simple Seduction

Minor 12–18, single/widow → arresto mayor.

Articles CCCXXXIX–CCXLI. Lascivious Acts and Corruption

Lascivious acts with consent → arresto mayor;
Corruption of minors → prision correccional min/med + temporary disqualification if public officer;
White slave trade → prision correccional med/max.

CHAPTER FOUR — Abduction

Article CCCXLII. Forcible Abduction

Reclusion temporal.

Article CCCXLIII. Consented Abduction (Minor 12–18)

Prision correccional min/med.

CHAPTER FIVE — Prosecution & Civil Liability

Article CCCXLIV. Prosecution

Requires complaint by offended party or guardian; marriage extinguishes criminal liability.

Article CCCXLV. Civil Liability

Indemnity, offspring support/acknowledgment.

Article CCCXLVI. Accomplices in Authority

Punished as principals + special disqualification.

TITLE TWELVE — Crimes Against Civil Status

CHAPTER ONE — Simulation of Births / Usurpation

Article CCCXLVII. Simulation or Substitution of Child

Conceal/abandon → prision mayor + fine ≤1,000; professionals aiding → temporary disqualification.

Article CCCXLVIII. Usurpation of Civil Status

Prision mayor if fraudulent; else prision correccional med/max.

CHAPTER TWO — Illegal Marriages

Article CCCXLIX. Bigamy

Prision mayor.

Article CCCL. Marriage Violating Legal Requirements

Prision correccional med/max; if consent obtained by force/fraud → max.

Article CCCLI. Premature Marriages (Widow/Pregnant)

Arresto mayor + fine ≤500 Bris.

Article CCCLII. Performing Illegal Marriage

Penalties under marriage law.

TITLE THIRTEEN — Crimes Against Honor

CHAPTER ONE — Libel

Articles CCCLIII–CCCLIV. Libel

Malicious imputation to dishonor another; presumption of malice unless privileged.

Articles CCCLV–CCCLVII. Written/Published Libel

Prision correccional min/med + fine 200–6,000 Bris; reporting private facts → arresto mayor/fine 200–2,000.

Articles CCCLVIII–CCCLIX. Oral Defamation

Arresto mayor → prision correccional min; minor → arresto menor/fine ≤200.

CHAPTER TWO — Incriminatory Machinations

Articles CCCLXIII–CCCLXIV. Falsely Incriminating

Arresto mayor; intrigues to blemish honor → arresto menor/fine ≤200.

TITLE FOURTEEN — Quasi-Offenses

Article CCCLXV. Criminal Negligence

  • Reckless imprudence → arresto mayor max → prision correccional min (grave felony)
  • Simple imprudence → arresto mayor med/max (grave), min (less serious)
  • Property damage → fine 1×–3× damage, min 25 Bris
  • Certain exemptions: if penalty ≤ standard, or under Auto Law → prision correccional med/max

TITLE FIFTEEN — Final Provisions

Article CCCLXVI. Felonies/Misdemeanors Prior to this Code

Punished under previous laws.

Article CCCLXVII. Repealing Clause

Repeals prior Penal Code, Provisional Law, specific Acts, and conflicting provisions.
