Preamble

We, the people of the Empire of Bristevia, in order to promote local **autonomy**, encourage participatory governance, ensure efficient delivery of basic services, and uphold **transparency and accountability**, enact this Local Government Code to guide the organization, powers, and responsibilities of local government units across the Imperial Realm.

TITLE I — GENERAL PROVISIONS

Chapter 1 — Definition of Terms

  1. **Local Government Unit (LGU)** — refers to the provinces, cities, municipalities (town), and communities within Bristevia.
  2. **Governor / Mayor / Community Chief** — the chief executive officer of the respective LGU.
  3. **Provincial Council / City Council / Municipal or Council / Community Council** — the legislative body of the LGU.
  4. **Local Development Council (LDC)** — a body composed of LGU officials and sectoral representatives that formulates development plans and programs.
  5. **Autonomy** — the power of an LGU to govern its affairs independently within the limits of national law.

TITLE II — STRUCTURE OF LOCAL GOVERNMENT

Chapter 2 — Levels of Local Government

  1. **Provinces**
    • Each province is headed by a **Governor** and a **Provincial Council**.
    • Provinces coordinate inter-municipal programs and provide technical assistance to municipalities and communities.
  2. **Cities**
    • Cities may be **1st class** or **2nd class** cities.
    • Each city is headed by a **City Mayor** and a **City Council**.
  3. **Municipalities**
    • Municipalities are headed by a **Municipal Mayor** and a **Municipal Council**.
    • Municipalities coordinate local services, infrastructure, and community programs.
  4. **Communities**
    • The smallest LGU unit, headed by a **Community Chief** and a **Community Council**.
    • Communities implement local programs, maintain civil registries, and manage neighborhood-level services.

Chapter 3 — Composition of Local Legislative Bodies

  • Each Provincial, City, Municipal, and Community Council shall consist of **elected members** as determined by population and administrative guidelines.
  • **Sectoral representatives** (youth, indigenous communities, marginalized sectors) shall comprise at least **20%** of each legislative body.

TITLE III — POWERS AND FUNCTIONS

Chapter 4 — General Powers of LGUs

  1. **Legislative Powers**
    • Enact ordinances and resolutions for the welfare of constituents.
    • Approve budgets, development plans, and taxation measures.
    • Regulate local business, commerce, and community services.
  2. **Executive Powers**
    • Implement laws, ordinances, and programs.
    • Deliver basic services including health, education, sanitation, infrastructure, and disaster management.
    • Maintain public order and safety in cooperation with national agencies.
  3. **Fiscal Powers**
    • Levy local taxes, fees, and charges in accordance with national law.
    • Manage LGU revenues, assets, and resources.
    • Approve local budgets and development funds.
  4. **Judicial/Quasi-Judicial Powers**
    • Resolve administrative disputes within the LGU jurisdiction.
    • Exercise disciplinary action over officials and employees under their authority.

Chapter 5 — Special Powers for Provinces and Cities

  • Provinces and independent cities may establish **economic zones, industrial parks, and tourism programs**.
  • They may enter into cooperative agreements with foreign or local entities with approval from the **Imperial Cabinet**.

TITLE IV — LOCAL PLANNING AND DEVELOPMENT

Chapter 6 — Local Development Councils (LDCs)

  • All LGUs shall establish an **LDC** to formulate and implement a Comprehensive Local Development Plan (**CLDP**).
  • LDCs must conduct **public consultations** before adoption of the CLDP.
  • Annual performance reports of LDCs shall be submitted to the **Imperial Ministry of Provincial Administration**.

Chapter 7 — Citizen Participation

LGUs shall facilitate:

  1. Public hearings on budgets and ordinances.
  2. Citizen assemblies for local development feedback.
  3. Participatory monitoring of local projects.

TITLE V — ACCOUNTABILITY AND OVERSIGHT

Chapter 8 — Internal Accountability

  • LGU officials shall submit **quarterly and annual reports** on programs, expenditures, and projects.
  • Community officials shall maintain a **Public Ledger** accessible to all residents.

Chapter 9 — Oversight Mechanisms

  • The Imperial Ministry of Provincial Administration may **audit LGU operations** and recommend corrective measures.
  • Citizens may file complaints or grievances regarding LGU malfeasance through the **Local Imperial Knight Office**.

Chapter 10 — Ethics and Conduct

LGU officials must adhere to a Code of Conduct:

  • No misuse of funds.
  • Transparency in procurement and project implementation.
  • Zero tolerance for corruption, nepotism, and abuse of power.

TITLE VI — TRANSITION AND IMPLEMENTATION

  1. All existing LGUs shall **reorganize** according to this Code within immediate enactment.
  2. Provincial and city ordinances inconsistent with this Code shall be **reviewed and harmonized**.
  3. The Imperial Ministry of Local Affairs shall issue **implementing rules and regulations (IRR)** within six months of enactment.

TITLE VII — MISCELLANEOUS PROVISIONS

  1. LGUs may establish partnerships with NGOs, private organizations, and other LGUs to enhance services.
  2. LGUs shall respect **indigenous practices and customary laws** unless conflicting with this Code or Imperial law.
  3. Violations of this Code may result in **suspension, fines, or removal from office** by the Imperial Court.

Additional Operational Guidelines

  • **Budget Approval:** Provincial and city councils approve budgets annually. Municipal and community councils approve budgets semi-annually.
  • **Development Planning:** Each LGU shall prepare a Comprehensive Local Development Plan (CLDP) every 3 years. CLDP must include infrastructure, health, education, disaster management, and revenue projections.
  • **Citizen Oversight:** Citizens have the right to attend council sessions, access public ledgers, and participate in project monitoring committees.
  • **Auditing:** The Imperial Ministry of Local Affairs audits LGUs annually. Misuse of funds triggers corrective action, including fines, suspension, or removal.
